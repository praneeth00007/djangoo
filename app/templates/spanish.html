How to Learn Spanish

If you’d like to learn Spanish, Lingoda is a great place to start. When learning any language, there are two main methods: working with a teacher in a group or individual class, or self-study, using books, online resources, and apps. The more time you invest in learning Spanish, the quicker you will learn the language. There are four main elements to language: listening, speaking, reading, and writing. To learn a language well, it’s essential to cover these four bases.

Working with a teacher provides structure for language learning and a step-by-step process. This is particularly beneficial for beginners, as your teacher will guide you based on your language level and learning style.

For self-study, there are plenty of resources online about how to learn Spanish effectively. Many language apps can also help you practice vocabulary and reading skills.

Is Spanish Hard to Learn?

Generally, Spanish is considered one of the easier languages for learners, especially if you speak a Romance language like French or Italian. However, it can be challenging for learners who speak languages that are not closely related to Spanish.

Many learners experience difficulty in pronunciation and verb conjugation, which can be quite different from English. The key is to practice regularly and immerse yourself in the language.

What Spanish Learning Levels are There?

In general, language learners are placed on the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) scale, which breaks down proficiency into four levels: A, B, C and D.

    A: Beginner
    B: Elementary
    C: Intermediate
    D: Upper-Intermediate

For your first lesson, you will take a placement test so that we can recommend the most suitable level for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long will it take to learn Spanish?▼
The time it takes to learn Spanish depends on several factors, including your previous language experience and the time you dedicate to practice. Generally, reaching a conversational level can take around 6 months to 2 years.
Can I learn Spanish without a teacher?▼
Yes, many people successfully learn Spanish through self-study using books, apps, and online resources. However, having a teacher can provide structure and feedback.
What resources are best for learning Spanish?▼
Resources such as language learning apps (like Duolingo or Babbel), online courses, podcasts, and Spanish-language media can be effective for learning Spanish.
