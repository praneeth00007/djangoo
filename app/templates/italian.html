How to Learn Italian

If you’d like to learn Italian, Lingoda is a great place to start. When learning any language, there are two main methods: working with a teacher in a group or individual class, or self-study, using books, online resources, and apps. The more time you invest in learning Italian, the quicker you will learn the language. There are four main elements to language: listening, speaking, reading, and writing. To learn a language well, it’s essential to cover these four bases.

Working with a teacher provides structure for language learning and a step-by-step process. This is particularly beneficial for beginners, as your teacher will guide you based on your language level and learning style.

For self-study, there are plenty of resources online about how to learn Italian effectively. Many language apps can also help you practice vocabulary and reading skills.

Is Italian an easy language to learn?

Everybody learns differently! There is no ‘one size fits all’ when it comes to learning Italian; it depends on the time you invest. Italian is a romance language derived from Latin.

English speakers sometimes find Italian tricky as the grammar, sentence structure, and vocabulary are not as similar.

However, this has not stopped millions of English speakers from learning Italian! It’s all about studying consistently and getting as much speaking practice as you can.

What Italian Learning Levels are There?

In general, language learners are placed on the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) scale, which breaks down proficiency into four levels: A, B, C and D.

    A: Beginner
    B: Elementary
    C: Intermediate
    D: Upper-Intermediate

For your first lesson, you will take a placement test so that we can recommend the most suitable level for you.
